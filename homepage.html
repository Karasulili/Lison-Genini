CREATURES AND REMNANTS

This project takes the form of a textile flag, a vestige of an imaginary civilisation in which extraterrestrial jellyfish played a central role. Through drawings, pictograms and an enigmatic typography, this work questions the transmission and erasure of cultures. The fabric, deliberately marked by time, becomes a narrative surface where each motif symbolises hypothetical narratives: interstellar migrations, bioluminescent networks and alliances between species. This flag, at the crossroads of myth and science fiction, invites us to speculate on alternative modes of communication and existence. Both a poetic and anthropological representation, the work embodies the fragility and persistence of memories in an ever-changing universe.

"Untilted", fabric painting, typography and pictograms, ESAL, 2025, 90x130 cm

IRON-LEGGED JELLYFISH

The iron-legged jellyfish embody a dense and poetic fictional universe, the fruit of around a hundred experiments. This project explores the construction of a complete civilisation: species, means of communication, languages, writing, and even modes of reproduction and development. The volumetric rendering conveys the fragile, icy essence of these imaginary creatures. The structures evoke a wandering that is both beautiful and painful, resonating with the state of flow. Their materiality combines lightness and density, between transparency and rust, underlining their duality: beings that are both ephemeral and mechanical, fragile and resilient. These creations are part of a reflection on the creation of imaginary ecosystems and the link between fiction and organic sensibility.

"Jambes de fer", porcelain and iron lines, ESAL, 2024, 50x120x40 cm

RECTO VERSO

This textile experiment explores the natural phenomena of erosion and capillarity through the application of paint on fabric. The coloured flows created by the interaction between the material and the liquid mimic the dynamics of water infiltrating soil or stone. Each trace, the result of an unpredictable process, reveals an organic aesthetic in which notions of control and chance intertwine. This work, at once technical and poetic, opens up avenues for reflection on the transformation of materials and the way in which natural forces sculpt our environment. As an essay, this piece provides a platform for future developments, exploring dialogues between matter, process and temporality.

"OVB", fabric painting, ISBA, 2023, 250x320 cm

EROSION

This series of four fabrics reflects on traces and the passage of time, combining capillarity, gravity and the interaction between liquid and matter. Through layers of juice of varying transparency, the fabrics reveal the imprints of objects, movements on the floor and folds, evidence of a silent dialogue between tool, surface and environment. Each piece captures the action of the liquid that shapes the fabric, revealing unpredictable, organic patterns. The accumulated strata evoke a constantly evolving temporality, where the material works autonomously to reveal poetic and sensitive compositions. These experiments open up perspectives on how natural processes and human gestures come together to generate unique visual narratives.

"Garden Floor", fabric painting, ISBA, 2024, 270x300 cm

CYANOCLIP

It's a video performance in which gesture and material interact to reveal imaginary marine forms inspired by limestone chancels. Through a layered approach, the cyanotype becomes a terrain for experimentation: paint, drawing gum and superimpositions intermingle, each stage contributing to the construction of a hybrid universe. This is a series of around fifty experiments. The performance withdrawal of the drawing gum, captured on video, reveals residues and traces that bear witness to the process. These fragments, both ephemeral and essential, embody the interplay between control and accident. In this production, cyanotype is not just a technique, but a tool for materialising an imaginary aquatic bestiary. *Cyanoclip* questions transformation, imprinting and the poetry of repeated gestures.
